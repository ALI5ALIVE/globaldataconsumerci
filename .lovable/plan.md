

# Plan: Rewrite Narration Scripts to World-Class Sales Standard

## Philosophy

Apply three principles from elite B2B selling:
1. **Open with tension, not introduction** — earn the next 5 minutes in the first 10 seconds
2. **Vary cadence** — alternate between punchy data hits and story-driven pauses
3. **Close with inevitability, not politeness** — make the next step feel like the only logical move

## Proposed Script Rewrites (19 slides, ~840 words total)

### Slide 0 — Title (10 words)
Drop the "welcome to" formality. One line that sets the frame:
*"Connected Intelligence. The unfair advantage your competitors are already building."*

### Slide 1 — Hook (55 words)
Lead with the uncomfortable truth. Name the tension explicitly:
*"Your teams have more data than ever — and less confidence to act on it. Consumer behaviour shifts three times faster than your insight cycles. The brands pulling ahead aren't buying more data. They're connecting the intelligence they have into a single system of conviction. That's what we're here to show you."*

### Slide 2 — Journey (35 words)
Shorter. Frame as "here's why this matters to you," not an agenda:
*"Nine minutes. Nine proof points. From the market pressure you're already feeling, through the intelligence gap costing you growth, to the platform that closes it. Let's move."*

### Slide 3 — Market Speed (65 words)
Keep the protein snacking story but make it visceral. Add the emotional cost:
*"Markets reward speed and punish hesitation. In 2023, a top-five CPG company saw the protein snacking wave building — but their teams had three conflicting views on sizing. Twelve weeks of reconciliation. By the time they aligned, Grenade and Barebells owned the shelf. The data existed. The alignment didn't. That gap cost them the category."*

### Slide 4 — Intelligence Gap (55 words)
Less list, more diagnosis. Make the buyer feel it:
*"We call it the Intelligence Gap — and it shows up in four ways. Conflicting sources. Missed launch windows. Twelve-week decision cycles. And the hardest one: teams that have the data but lack the conviction to act. One top-ten company spent twelve weeks reconciling numbers for a single quarterly review. That's not analysis. That's paralysis."*

### Slide 5 — Transformation (50 words)
Sharpen the before/after contrast. Make "after" feel tangible:
*"Ferrero faced exactly this. Five data vendors. Twelve weeks to a decision. Siloed teams debating numbers instead of acting on them. Today: one platform, days to decision, every team seeing the same truth. Five vendors replaced. Thirty percent lower cost. Same data — finally connected."*

### Slides 6-10 — Intelligence Wheel (45 words each, varied structure)
Break the repetitive pattern. Alternate between "pain → rescue" and "imagine if":
- **Strategic**: Start with the cost of being late — *"Fifty million euros. That's what a rushed reformulation cost a top-five beverage company. They saw the regulatory shift — six months too late..."*
- **Market**: Start with the board moment — *"Picture this: your board asks for a twenty-five million euro commitment. You need ninety percent confidence in the market sizing. Where does that number come from?"*
- **Competitive**: Start with the threat — *"Your competitor just reformulated their hero SKU. You have six weeks before it hits shelf. What do you do?"*
- **Innovation**: Start with the waste — *"Sixty percent of new product concepts fail within twelve months. Not because the ideas were bad — because validation came too late..."*
- **Sales**: Start with the buyer meeting — *"You're pitching Tesco next week. What story lands with that specific buyer? Not your story. Theirs."*

### Slides 11-14 — Maturity (40 words each)
Reframe as a mirror. Open each with: *"If this sounds familiar, you're here..."*
Make the buyer self-locate and feel the pull toward the next stage.

### Slide 15 — Why GlobalData (50 words)
Drop the list of three. Instead, answer the unspoken objection: *"You're thinking — we've heard this before. Here's what's different..."*

### Slide 16 — The Return (45 words)
Lead with the name, not the metric: *"Mondelēz. Six weeks to days. Two times launch success. Thirty percent lower total cost. Not projected — measured."*

### Slide 17 — Get Connected (40 words)
Create urgency through loss, not features: *"Every week you stay fragmented, your competitors compound their advantage. Ninety days. That's all it takes to move from five vendors to one platform."*

### Slide 18 — CTA (25 words)
End with inevitability: *"The only question is whether you lead or follow. Let's have the conversation that decides which. Thank you."*
